Greatest Common Divisor (GCD)

Write a program to compute the GCD of two positive integers using Euclid's algorithm. The subtraction-based version of the algorithm (summarised below) is probably the easiest version to understand and implement, initially. Tips: The GCD of two positive integers is the largest positive integer that divides them both (remainder zero). In summary: Euclid's method for computing the GCD of two positive integers consists of replacing the larger number by the difference of the numbers, and repeating this until the two numbers are equal. When the two numbers have become equal, the algorithm stops, and this equality is the GCD of the original two numbers.
